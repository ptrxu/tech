Signal Strength To view on any cellular account do the following

  1. Find account under "Device Status" tab

  2. Scroll to "Current Status" and click on QOS (Quality Of Service) tab

  3. Any previous QOS signal strength data appears

  4. Select "Current Status" and under "Ping Commands" select "QOS (Status)" then "Ping Device"

  5. AlarmNet will query account and display new QOS data under QOS tab

Data will look like this

Received GMT = When data was received in Greenwich Mean Time (6 hours ahead of CST)

Line Voltage = DC voltage within radio not AC to the terminals, should be between 9 to 16.5V

(QOS voltage is AC voltage at terminals x 1.414)

Battery = Battery voltage, should be above 6V, low battery condition if 5.7V +/-5%

if 0000 battery is either disabled (if LynxR-I) or missing

Receiver Level = Average signal strength heard during QOS period

Min/Max Level = Minimum/Maximum signals strength from one QOS check to the next

Signal strength is measured in dB and will always be a negative number from -50 to -100dB, a lower number is better

(Example: -56db is better than -62db, anything higher than -94 is unacceptable)

2 solid yellows and 3 solid greens = about -75 db

2 solid yellows and 2 solid greens = about -85 db
